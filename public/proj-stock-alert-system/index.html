Stock Alert System

arsenniyet4/stock-alert-system

About the Project

Stock Alert System is a Python-based market monitoring service that continuously polls live price data from Yahoo Finance and evaluates a set of configurable buy and sell signals. When a signal is triggered, the system dispatches a formatted email alert to one or more subscribers via the Brevo SMTP relay. The whole stack runs unattended on a Google Cloud e2-micro VM (free tier), making it a zero-cost, always-on solution for keeping an eye on the markets.

The system supports multiple alert types — including real-time price action notifications and a daily end-of-day snapshot — giving subscribers a clear, at-a-glance view of what the market is doing without needing to check manually.

Price Action Alert

When the system detects a significant price movement or a buy/sell signal for a monitored ticker, it immediately fires an email notification to all configured subscribers. The alert includes the ticker symbol, current price, and the nature of the signal — giving you instant awareness of market moves without constantly watching a screen.

An email alert delivered directly to a subscriber's inbox, showing a triggered price action signal from the monitoring service.

Daily Price Snapshot

Once a day the system compiles a full summary of all monitored stocks and sends it as a structured HTML email. Each row in the snapshot shows the ticker, the current price, and the daily price change, giving subscribers a concise digest of the entire watchlist in a single glance. This eliminates the need to open a brokerage app every morning just to check where things stand.

The daily snapshot email listing all monitored tickers with their latest prices and percentage changes for the session.

Detailed Signal Summary

For individual tickers that trigger a signal, the system sends a rich, detailed summary email. This includes a breakdown of the signal type, historical context, and key price levels — giving the recipient everything they need to evaluate whether to act on the alert. This is especially useful for swing-trade setups where context around the signal matters as much as the signal itself.
